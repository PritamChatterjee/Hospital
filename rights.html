Patients Rights & Concerns



Cambridge Hospital's goal is to provide the best health care experience possible. Patients or family members can share their compliments or voice any concerns about their experience by using the resources listed on this page.

The Cambridge Hospital encourages all patients and families to report any issues to the hospital through the following resources.

Patients Rights

Cambridge Hospital is committed to maintaining the rights, dignity and well-being of all patients. Learn about your rights as a patient and how Mass General can provide you with the best possible care.

Cambridge Hospital does not discriminate against any individual, regardless of race, color, religious creed, gender, gender identity or expression, genetic information, sexual orientation, age, disability, veteran or active military status, marital status or national origin.


Office of Patient Advocacy

The Office of Patient Advocacy provides a link between you and Cambridge Hospital leadership. Patient advocates will communicate your compliments or investigate any complaint you might have.


Statement About Doctors' Relationships
with Outside Companies (Industry)

Many Partners HealthCare (Partners) doctors work closely with companies (industry) that make drugs, medical equipment or provide other products and services. When our doctors work with these companies, it results in the development of new and better ways to diagnose and treat illness, and benefits the health of the public.

Sometimes the companies pay Partners HealthCare or Partners doctors for services, such as consulting or giving educational talks. Doctors may also own stock in these companies. We review all of these relationships to make sure our policy is followed so that your care is not affected in any way.
